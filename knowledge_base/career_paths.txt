CAREER PATHS IN DATA SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY
==========================================

TYPICAL CAREER PROGRESSION TIMELINES
--------------------------------------

Data Analyst Path:
  Junior Data Analyst (0-2 years)
    → Data Analyst (2-4 years)
      → Senior Data Analyst (4-6 years)
        → Lead Analyst / Analytics Manager (6-9 years)
          → Head of Analytics / Director of Analytics (9+ years)

Data Scientist Path:
  Junior Data Scientist (0-2 years)
    → Data Scientist (2-5 years)
      → Senior Data Scientist (5-8 years)
        → Lead / Principal Data Scientist (8-12 years)
          → Director of Data Science / VP of Data (12+ years)
            → Chief Data Officer (CDO) (15+ years)

Machine Learning Engineer Path:
  Junior ML Engineer (0-2 years)
    → ML Engineer (2-5 years)
      → Senior ML Engineer (5-8 years)
        → Staff ML Engineer (8-12 years)
          → Principal ML Engineer (12+ years)
            → Distinguished Engineer / Fellow (rare, 15+ years)

Data Engineer Path:
  Junior Data Engineer (0-2 years)
    → Data Engineer (2-5 years)
      → Senior Data Engineer (5-8 years)
        → Staff / Principal Data Engineer (8-12 years)
          → Head of Data Engineering (10+ years)

Research Scientist Path (Academic):
  PhD Student (4-6 years)
    → Postdoctoral Researcher (2-4 years)
      → Assistant Professor / Research Scientist (varies)
        → Associate Professor / Senior Researcher
          → Full Professor / Distinguished Researcher

Research Scientist Path (Industry):
  Research Intern / Resident
    → Research Scientist (PhD + 0-3 years)
      → Senior Research Scientist (3-7 years)
        → Principal Research Scientist (7-12 years)
          → Distinguished Scientist / VP Research (12+ years)

CAREER TRANSITIONS
-------------------

From Data Analyst to Data Scientist:
- Bridge the gap by learning machine learning (scikit-learn, model evaluation)
- Build a portfolio of ML projects on Kaggle or personal datasets
- Learn Python deeply if currently focused on SQL/BI tools
- Take courses: fast.ai, Andrew Ng's ML Specialisation on Coursera
- Typical transition time: 1-2 years with deliberate upskilling
- Entry point: Often through "Analytics Engineer" or hybrid analyst/scientist roles

From Data Analyst to Data Engineer:
- Focus on Python programming and SQL at scale
- Learn workflow orchestration tools (Airflow, dbt)
- Build ETL pipeline projects (even personal ones count)
- Learn cloud data services (BigQuery, Redshift, S3)
- Typical transition time: 1-2 years

From Software Engineer to ML Engineer:
- Add ML fundamentals: take Andrew Ng's ML course, fast.ai
- Learn Python data stack if coming from other languages
- Build projects that combine software engineering with ML models
- Strongest transition path due to existing engineering skills
- Typical transition time: 6-18 months

From Data Scientist to ML Engineer:
- Strengthen software engineering practices (clean code, testing, CI/CD)
- Learn containerisation (Docker, Kubernetes)
- Gain experience with production systems and MLOps tools
- Focus on the "engineering" aspect: scalability, reliability, performance
- Typical transition time: 1-2 years

From Data Scientist to Research Scientist:
- Pursue a PhD or contribute to open-source ML research
- Publish or contribute to academic papers
- Deep specialisation in a subfield (NLP, CV, RL, etc.)
- Work on novel problems rather than applied business problems
- Very competitive without a PhD from a top institution

From Academic Research to Industry:
- Highlight transferable skills: Python, statistics, problem-solving, publication record
- Focus on applied ML roles at companies with research divisions (Google, Meta, Microsoft)
- Be prepared to adapt to product-focused goals rather than pure research

INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTOR (IC) VS MANAGEMENT TRACKS
--------------------------------------------------

IC Track (Technical Leadership):
- Staff Engineer / Senior Scientist → Principal → Distinguished → Fellow
- Deep technical expertise, cross-team technical influence
- Architecture decisions, setting technical standards
- Mentoring without direct reports
- Preferred by those who want to stay hands-on with technology

Management Track:
- Senior Scientist/Engineer → Tech Lead → Manager → Director → VP → C-Suite
- People management, strategic planning, hiring
- Cross-functional collaboration and stakeholder management
- Removed from day-to-day coding as seniority increases
- Requires strong communication, EQ, and organisational skills

Most companies offer both tracks at large organisations (Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft). At startups, the distinction is often blurred.

ENTRY PATHS INTO DATA SCIENCE
-------------------------------

University Degree:
- Computer Science, Statistics, Mathematics, Physics, Economics
- A degree is still the most common path but not the only one
- Graduate programmes (MSc, PhD) accelerate the path to senior roles

Bootcamps and Online Courses:
- Effective for career changers with some technical background
- Must be supplemented with a strong portfolio
- Popular programmes: Turing College, Springboard, BrainStation, General Assembly
- Credibility comes from the portfolio, not the bootcamp brand

Self-Taught:
- Requires extreme discipline and a strong portfolio
- Online resources: fast.ai, Kaggle, Towards Data Science, Papers With Code
- Most successful when combined with real projects or contributions to open source

Kaggle Competitions:
- Excellent for building ML skills and visibility
- Top placements are highly valued by ML-focused employers
- Kaggle Notebooks demonstrate practical skills

Internal Transitions:
- Moving from a business analyst, software engineer, or finance role within the same company
- Often underrated — internal knowledge + new skills = strong value proposition

SPECIALISATION AREAS
---------------------

Natural Language Processing (NLP) / Large Language Models:
- Extremely high demand in 2024-2025 due to the GenAI revolution
- Skills: Transformers, HuggingFace, fine-tuning, RAG, LangChain
- Roles: NLP Engineer, LLM Engineer, AI Engineer, Conversational AI

Computer Vision:
- Strong demand in manufacturing, healthcare, autonomous vehicles, retail
- Skills: CNNs, object detection (YOLO), segmentation, image generation
- Roles: Computer Vision Engineer, Perception Engineer

Reinforcement Learning:
- Niche but growing (robotics, gaming, recommendation systems, AlphaFold)
- Requires strong mathematical foundations
- Roles: RL Researcher, Robotics ML Engineer

Time Series & Forecasting:
- Finance, supply chain, energy, retail
- Skills: ARIMA, Prophet, temporal fusion transformers, LSTMs
- Roles: Quantitative Analyst, Forecasting Data Scientist

Recommendation Systems:
- E-commerce, streaming, social media
- Skills: Collaborative filtering, matrix factorisation, deep learning-based RecSys
- Roles: Recommendation Systems Engineer, Personalisation Scientist

MLOps / AI Platform:
- High demand as companies scale their ML infrastructure
- Skills: Kubernetes, MLflow, feature stores, model monitoring
- Roles: MLOps Engineer, ML Platform Engineer, AI Infrastructure Engineer

COMPANY TYPES AND THEIR IMPACT ON CAREERS
------------------------------------------

Big Tech (Google, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple):
- Highest salaries and best benefits
- Access to the largest scale infrastructure and data
- Slower career progression due to competition
- Strong brand for future opportunities
- Highly structured levelling systems (L3-L10 at Google, etc.)

AI-First Companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral, Cohere, Hugging Face):
- Cutting-edge AI work, direct impact on AI advancement
- Competitive salaries, strong equity upside
- Fast-moving, less structured than big tech
- Excellent for building expertise in LLMs and generative AI

Mid-Size Tech Companies:
- Faster career progression (can reach senior/lead in 3-4 years)
- More ownership and autonomy over projects
- Good balance of compensation and growth

Startups (< 50 employees):
- High equity, lower base salary typically
- Wear many hats — great for learning breadth
- High risk, high reward
- May not have established data culture or infrastructure

Finance/Banking (Hedge funds, investment banks, FinTech):
- Quantitative roles among the highest paid in data science
- Competitive, highly structured, often require strong mathematics
- Quant roles at hedge funds can reach €200k-€500k+ for experienced professionals

Consulting (McKinsey QuantumBlack, Deloitte AI, Accenture AI):
- Exposure to diverse industries and problems
- Fast learning curve, good for career switchers
- Travel can be demanding
- Good stepping stone to industry roles
